The sauna is perhaps the oldest wellness facility in human history — and one of the most effective. The Finnish tradition of alternating dry heat with cooling has been practised for thousands of years and is now scientifically validated: regular sauna use improves cardiovascular health, accelerates detoxification, reduces muscle soreness and enhances sleep quality.

At Pools4You we design and install saunas for every context — from a compact home cabin to a prestigious multi-room wellness complex.

## Types of Saunas

**Finnish Dry Sauna** — The classic. Temperatures between 80 and 100 °C with low humidity (around 10–20%). The stone heater (kiuas) is the heart of the facility — water poured over the heated rocks creates a brief, intense burst of steam (löyly) that generates a sensation of powerful heat. The timber lining — typically alder, aspen or thermo-oak — absorbs moisture and remains pleasant to the touch even at high temperatures.

**Bio-Sauna (Soft Sauna)** — A more accessible alternative for those sensitive to extreme heat. Temperatures range from 50 to 65 °C at higher humidity (40–60%). The effect is gentler and suited to longer sessions. Particularly popular in hotel facilities, as it is comfortable for a wider guest audience.

**Infrared Sauna** — Rather than heating the air, infrared panels warm the body directly, penetrating deeper into the tissues. The temperature is lower (40–55 °C), but the therapeutic effect — especially for joint pain and muscular tension — is pronounced. Energy consumption is significantly lower than in a traditional sauna.

**Steam Room (Hammam)** — Moist heat at 40–50 °C and 100% humidity. Suitable for skin and respiratory treatments. It requires specific waterproof materials and precise vapour barriers — areas where our team has extensive experience.

**Panoramic and Glass Saunas** — When architecture is as important as function. Saunas with full glass walls integrated into interior or exterior designs with views of nature, cityscapes or internal courtyards.

**Outdoor Saunas (Barrel Saunas and Modular Cabins)** — Ideal for private gardens, mountain cabins, glamping sites and small hotels. The cylindrical form provides excellent thermal efficiency, while the timber exterior blends naturally into open-air settings.

Internal finishing uses resinous Nordic timbers with high heat resistance: alder, aspen, abachi, thermo-oak. Lighting uses low-voltage LED systems specifically designed for humid and thermal environments. Controls can be analogue or digital, with options for remote management and pre-programming.
